About Orionis

Orionis takes place in the year 2653 in the Milky Way galaxy. Humanity has arisen from Earth to discover the they are not alone in the universe, and that instead that the Galaxy is teeming with life. Humanity, now united under the Terran Federation of Sol, are only one of many advanced civilizations. The most powerful of these civilizations are: the High Flovok Imperium, the New Muraezi Diarchy, the Mondraic Conglomerate of Economic States, the United Cielaven Freeholds, and the Terran Federation of Sol.

At the start of each game, the player will choose which of the major factions they would like to represent. Each faction has different strengths, weaponry, and visual style. The player will start with a single ship, the flagship. The flagship is able to request reinforcements from the player's faction leaders (who might deny the player's requests if the player performs badly), and thus the player can add to their fleet(s) in order to destroy the enemy flagship, which is the goal of the game. However, if the player loses their flagship the rest of the player's ships will retreat leaving the enemy victorious.

The story of Orionis will focus on the tension in the Galaxy caused by entire systems going dark with no warning. The High Flovok Imperium blames the New Muraezi Diarchy, being that the Diarchy is the most advanced of the large civilizations. However, the Terran Federation of Sol and the United Cielaven Freeholds blame the Imperium due to the Imperium's past incursions. The Imperium threatens war against the Diarchy if another one of their systems goes silent. One week later, the Imperium capital of Moska Imper suddenly stops recieving communications from Kal Trand, a major Imperium port.

Orionis is coded in Java using the LibGDX library. The game is estimated to be roughly 15% complete.

Notice

The current version of Orionis is not (exactly) playable. The pre-flagship version of the game can be found here, which is currently more playable though plays differently than the gameplay that is planned for the post-flagship version of the game.

To play either version of the game, Java 1.8+ must be installed. To play the post-flagship version of the game, Gradle must be installed.

It is recommended to play either version of the game in fullscreen mode to minimize any stuttering. It appears to be a limitation of openGL / LibGDX as I cannot find a fix or any suggestion of faulty programming on my part, but of course I could be simply overlooking said faulty programming. If you see a possible cause on my part, please let me know (see my resume on the main page for my email address).
